Google play store data

This is a report based on Kaggle dataset about Google play store application. The dataset contained NaN, missing values or invalid strings, therefore we had to clean it first by using Python libraries such as Pandas and Numpy (View notebook).
It is possible to see a sample below.

First we looked at the distribution of ratings: most of the users rate an application between 4.3 and 4.5. Furthermore, it is noticeable how an user who enjoy an app tend to rate it directly with 5 instead of picking up a more thoughtful rate.

Categories Analysis

We can dive into our data to gain more interesting information by looking at the mean of some features grouped by category
First, by using a boxplot, we can show how ratings are distributed among categories:

Health and Fitness and Events are the categories with the highest median value (the best developed). On the other hand, Dating is the category with the lowest median value. It might probably demonstrate that these apps are not developed as good as the applications belonging to the former categories.

We can now use bar charts to show how different features ( i.e. number of download, size of the app, app prices, and number of reviews) are distributed among categories.

These graphs show that games are predominant in the Google Play store. Although they have on average a big size, they still are the apps downloaded the most and, as expected, with the highest number of reviews. By looking at the Categories Vs Price bar chart, we can notice that Finance and Lifestyle applications are the most expensive. Hence, they do not have a big number of downloads and reviews.
